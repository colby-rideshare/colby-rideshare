Support

How it works (as a driver):

  • You post a ride using the form in the Drivers page
  • A Google Maps display of your route is then displayed for other Colby students to see under the Riders page
  • If another student needs to get somewhere along your route, they can submit a ride request, which asks them to enter their trip details
  • We email you the ride request, showing you a side-by-side Google Maps view of your old route and hypothetical route if you were to accept (along with how much time it would add you)
  • Simply accept or decline the ride request with the click of a button -- we notify the rider for you so there's no need to do anything else

How it works (as a rider):

  • Navigate to the Riders page to view trip details posted by other Colby students
  • Filter your search results to find rides that are leaving on a day/time when you want to leave
  • Find a ride going to a similar place as you (or passing through where you need to go)
  • Select the More Info button, where you'll have the option to send a customized message to the driver and enter your trip details
  • We'll pass your message along to the driver and get back to you as soon as we hear back from the driver



About Us

Colby Rideshare was created by two Colby students who struggled time and time again to find rides to and from campus. We wanted to create a platform to help all members of the Colby community travel to and from campus seamlessly. We believe that whether you're looking for a ride to Portland Jetport or just looking for people to keep you company on a long drive back to New York, Colby Rideshare will bring us closer as a community and ease some of the stress that many students face when making travel plans.

Why should I offer to drive?

  • It's a kind gesture, and offering to drive someone along your way doesn't take much effort and helps the Colby community
  • Gas is expensive! You can ask your riders to split gas costs with you
  • Only fellow Colby students are allowed to join, and you might just become friends with someone new
  • Driving long distances has a major carbon footprint and by carpooling you're doing your part to minimize the impact on the planet
  • Ultimately, you're allowed to decline any ride request if you feel the ride is too far out of your way
